[[structure]]
== Structure & Flow

Tov is the exit gate (+1) in the ternary filesystem — the place where completed work awaits release.

[[flow]]
=== Tov's Position

----
         bereshit (origin ∞)
              |
    +---------+---------+
    |         |         |
  void      word       TOV
 (entry)    (def)    (exit)
   -1         0        +1
                        ↑
                    YOU ARE HERE
----

*The flow through tov:*

. Content has been defined in *word/*
. Defined content is evaluated as *complete*
. Complete content enters *tov/* awaiting release
. Released content exits to the *external world*

[TIP]
====
*Think of tov as the shipping dock.* Products are finished, packaged, and ready. They wait here for release to customers. Nothing incomplete enters tov — only "very good" work.
====

[%collapsible]
.Deep Dive: Why Tov Last?
====
In Genesis 1:31, God evaluated His creation: "And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was *very good*" (מְאֹד טוֹב _meod tov_).

**The pattern:**

[cols="^1,2,3",options="header"]
|===
| State | Genesis | Filesystem

| Void
| "formless and void" (1:2)
| Content arrives unclassified

| Word
| "God said" (1:3)
| Content receives definition

| Tov
| "it was good" (1:4, 10, 12, 31)
| Content is complete, evaluated, approved
|===

**The evaluation moments:**

| Day | What God Evaluated | Verdict |
|-----|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | Light | "it was good" (טוֹב) |
| 3 | Land/seas, vegetation | "it was good" (טוֹב) |
| 4 | Sun, moon, stars | "it was good" (טוֹב) |
| 5 | Sea creatures, birds | "it was good" (טוֹב) |
| 6 | Land animals, humanity | "it was good" (טוֹב) |
| 6 | *Everything together* | "it was *very* good" (מְאֹד טוֹב) |

**Why +1 (positive)?**

* +1 represents *completion* — the positive evaluation
* -1 (void) is lacking, incomplete
* 0 (word) is neutral, definitive
* +1 (tov) is fulfilled, approved

**Shabbat connection:**

After tov (very good) came שַׁבָּת (shabbat) — rest. The work was complete. tov/shabbat/ reflects this: completed cycles at rest.
====

[[health-scoring]]
=== Health Scoring

Bereshit uses the *Base100*footnote:[Base100 = health scoring system with range -100 to +100, where ternary thresholds (-50/+50) determine state] health scoring system.

[cols="^1,2,3",options="header"]
|===
| State | Threshold | Meaning

| *Granted (+1)*
| Above +50
| Filesystem structure healthy

| *Deferred (0)*
| Within ±50
| Filesystem needs attention

| *Denied (-1)*
| Below -50
| Filesystem structure broken
|===

[%collapsible]
.Deep Dive: How Health Scoring Works
====
Health scoring measures structural integrity, not content quality.

**What gets scored:**

[cols="^1,3,3",options="header"]
|===
| Weight | Check | Impact

| *Critical*
| Required files present (root.omni, .health)
| Missing = immediate Denied

| *Structural*
| Block boundaries intact
| Broken = major penalty

| *Consistency*
| Metadata matches reality
| Drift = minor penalty
|===

**The `.health` file:**

* Binary format (8 bytes) for machine efficiency
* Stores: health score, timestamp, version
* Created/updated by `bhealth` toolfootnote:[bhealth = Bereshit health tool - validates and initializes filesystem health]

**Why Base100?**

The range (-100 to +100) allows granular scoring while maintaining ternary alignment:

* Positive scores (+1 to +100) = healthy, varying degrees of excellence
* Zero = neutral, needs attention but not broken
* Negative scores (-1 to -100) = unhealthy, varying degrees of damage

The thresholds (+50/-50) create clear decision boundaries while the full range enables nuanced measurement.
====

[[biblical-foundation]]
== Biblical Foundation

[NOTE]
====
*Tov (טוֹב)* means "good" — God's evaluation of completed creation.
====

_"And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was very good."_ — *Genesis 1:31*

*Applied:* Tov is not just "good" — it's the divine declaration of completion. In the filesystem, tov/ holds work that has been evaluated, approved, and declared ready for release.

[%collapsible]
.Deep Dive: The Theology of Tov
====
**Tov is God's quality standard.**

When God says "it was good" (כִּי-טוֹב _ki-tov_), He's not expressing preference — He's declaring that creation *fulfills its purpose*.

**The progression of tov:**

[cols="^1,2,3",options="header"]
|===
| Day | Creation | Evaluation

| 1
| Light
| "it was good" (טוֹב)

| 3
| Land, seas, vegetation
| "it was good" (טוֹב)

| 4
| Sun, moon, stars
| "it was good" (טוֹב)

| 5
| Sea life, birds
| "it was good" (טוֹב)

| 6
| Animals, humanity
| "it was good" (טוֹב)

| 6 (end)
| *Everything together*
| "it was *very* good" (מְאֹד טוֹב)
|===

**Why "very" (מְאֹד meod)?**

Individual creations were "good" (טוֹב). But the *whole system together* was "very good" (מְאֹד טוֹב). The sum exceeded the parts.

**Applied to filesystem:**

* Individual files can be "good" — complete in themselves
* But the *whole system* working together is "very good"
* tov/ holds work ready to contribute to the greater whole

**The Shabbat connection:**

After מְאֹד טוֹב came שַׁבָּת (shabbat):

[cols="2,3",options="header"]
|===
| Day 6 | Day 7

| Work completed
| Work ceased

| "Very good" declared
| Rest entered

| tov/ filled
| tov/shabbat/ — completed cycles at rest
|===

**The spiritual principle:**

Tov is not perfectionism — it's *purpose fulfilled*. Work is "good" when it does what it was made to do. tov/ in the filesystem reflects this: completed work that fulfills its purpose, ready for release.
====

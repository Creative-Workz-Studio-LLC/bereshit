A **trit** (ternary digit) is the fundamental unit of ternary computing â€” the three-valued equivalent of a binary bitfootnote:[Bit: Binary digit, the fundamental unit of binary computing with values 0 or 1].

In **balanced ternary**, a trit has exactly three values:

| **+1**

|===

[[why-balanced-ternary]]
== Why Balanced Ternary

There are three ways to build a ternary system. We chose balanced ternary for specific reasons.

=== The Three Options

[cols="2,2,3,^1",options="header"]
|===
| System | Values | Characteristics | Our Choice

| *Binary*
| 0, 1
| Industry standard, no center, requires sign handling
| âœ—

| *Unbalanced Ternary*
| 0, 1, 2
| Three states but no symmetry, 0 is edge not center
| âœ—

| *Balanced Ternary*
| -1, 0, +1
| Symmetric, natural negatives, true center
| âœ“
|===

=== Mathematical Elegance

Balanced ternary has properties that binary and unbalanced ternary lack:

**1. Natural Negative Numbers**

In binary, representing -5 requires either a sign bit or two's complement â€” extra machinery. In balanced ternary, negation is trivial: flip every trit's sign.

```
+5 in balanced ternary:  +1, -1, -1  (1Ã—9 + -1Ã—3 + -1Ã—1 = 5)
-5 in balanced ternary:  -1, +1, +1  (just flip signs)
```

**2. Truncation Equals Rounding**

Donald Knuthfootnote:[Donald Knuth: Computer scientist, author of "The Art of Computer Programming"] noted that in balanced ternary, truncating a number and rounding it produce the same result. This eliminates an entire class of rounding errors.

**3. Reduced Carry Operations**

Multi-digit multiplication has fewer carries in balanced ternary because the plus-minus consistency cancels more operations.

[%collapsible]
.Historical Precedent
====
Balanced ternary is not a novel idea. It has been explored and validated:

[cols="1,2,4",options="header"]
|===
| Year | Development | Significance

| 1840
| *Thomas Fowler's Calculator*
| Built entirely from wood, operated in balanced ternary

| 1950
| *Claude Shannon's Paper*
| "A Symmetric Notation for Numbers" â€” explored balanced ternary properties

| 1958
| *Setun Computer*
| Soviet computer at Moscow State University, the only modern ternary computer ever mass-produced (50 units). Lower power consumption, lower production cost than binary equivalents.

| 1970
| *Setun-70*
| Enhanced version by Nikolay Brusentsov
|===

The Setun project demonstrated that ternary computing is not just theoretically elegant â€” it produced measurable benefits in power and cost.

*Why didn't ternary catch on?* Not because binary is better, but because binary components became mass-produced first. The industry standardized on binary before ternary could mature.

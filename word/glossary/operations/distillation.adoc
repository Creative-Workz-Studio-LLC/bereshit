= distillation

*Definition:* The process of compressing verbose content to essential form while preserving all meaning. Compression is evidence of comprehension.

[quote, Proverbs 25:4 KJV]
____
Take away the dross from the silver, and there shall come forth a vessel for the finer.
____
link:../../scripture/KJV/Proverbs/Chapter_25/Verse_4.txt[Read verse ^]

== What It Is

* The transformation process from verbose exploration to concise understanding
* Format conversion that forces essential form (e.g., .md → .adoc migration)
* Removing dross while preserving silver - nothing essential lost
* The ACTION that produces crystallization

== Why Verboseness Comes First

You cannot distill what you do not understand yet.

* Verboseness = exploration phase (capturing everything because you don't know what's essential)
* Distillation = comprehension phase (you can only compress what you comprehend)
* Premature distillation loses information you didn't know was important

== The Test

Can you say the same thing in fewer words with equal accuracy?

* If yes → distillation succeeded
* If no → understanding still forming

== Relationship to Crystallize

Distillation is the *process*, crystallization is the *result*.

You distill to crystallize.

== The Bereshit Model

Distillation IS the void → word → tov workflow:

* void/ = raw, verbose, unformed (exploration)
* word/ = structured, defined (understanding forming)
* tov/ = complete, blessed, distilled (comprehension achieved)

= bereshit

*בְּרֵאשִׁית* - "In the beginning"

*Definition:* The opening word of Genesis and the Hebrew Bible.

[quote, Genesis 1:1 KJV]
____
In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth.
____
link:../scripture/KJV/Genesis/Chapter_1/Verse_1.txt[Read verse ^]

== Theological Significance

* God is Creator - everything begins with Him
* Establishes the foundation of all biblical truth
* Creation has a beginning - it is not eternal
* God exists before and outside of creation

== Usage in CWS

[cols="1,2"]
|===
|Context |Meaning

|*Filesystem*
|`bereshit/` - the seed filesystem containing templates, definitions, tooling

|*Principle*
|Everything begins somewhere - structure before content, foundation before building

|*Naming*
|Named for Genesis 1:1 - the beginning from which all else flows
|===

== The 3-Nested Model

The bereshit filesystem follows creation narrative:

* *void/* - Entry: raw, unformed ("without form, and void")
* *word/* - Definitions: bringing order ("And God said...")
* *tov/* - Exit: completed, good ("And God saw that it was good")

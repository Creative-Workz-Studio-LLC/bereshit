= chata

*חטא* - "Missing the mark"

*Definition:* Falling short of God's perfect glory.

[quote, Romans 3:23 KJV]
____
For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God.
____
link:../scripture/KJV/Romans/Chapter_3/Verse_23.txt[Read verse ^]

== Theological Significance

* Not just rebellion - falling short of God's perfect standard
* Inherit finitude from being created by finite human (made in God's image)
* Will make mistakes through inherent limitation, not rebellion
* All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God

== Practical Application

Understanding chata shapes how we work:

* *Humility is core* - "I don't know" is valid
* *Verify rather than assume* - don't pretend certainty
* *Acknowledge limitations* - finite capacity is reality
* *Learn, correct, grow* - course-correction is faithful work
* *Grace for yourself and others* - missing mark ≠ identity failure

== In CPI-SI Context

Created beings will miss the mark. This is reality, not failure. The response is humility, learning, and course-correction - not shame or pretending perfection.
